Greater Kudu

  • About

    Greater Kudu are native to eastern and southern Africa. Their diets include flowers, fruits, leaves, tubers, and shoots. Greater kudu arethe second tallest antelope. Female greater kudu weigh from 265 pounds to 475 pounds. The males weigh from 420 pounds to 700 pounds and have horns that are usually are 4 feet tall. These horns also have 2.5 inch twists. Surprisingly, greater kudus can jump very high, with a record jump at over 8.25 feet. Their life span is around 15 years.

Plains Zebra

  • About

    Plains zebra are native to savannas and woodlands of eastern and southern Sub-Saharan Africa. Their diet consists of grass and leaves. Zebras have a unique coat that is full of stripes. Each zebra has a unique set of individual stripes, just like fingertips. And underneath their coat, their skin is all black. Another fact is that zebras usually have great hearing and eyesight. The average weight of a zebra is around 500 to 800 pounds. Even at this weight, they can run up to 35 miles per hour. Their life span is around 15 to 20 years.
