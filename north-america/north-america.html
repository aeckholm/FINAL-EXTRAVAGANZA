Caribou

  • About

    Caribou are native to the northern regions of Asia, Europe, Greenland, and North America. In North America, these animals are known as Caribou but in Europe they are referred to as Reindeer. Caribou travel north in herds in summer and are considered one of the largest animal migrations on Earth. Their primary diet is vegetation, and they dig for their food with their large hooves. The female caribous can weigh up to 300 pounds, and the male caribous can weigh up to 600 pounds.

Harbor Seal

  • About

    Harbor Seals are native to the North Atlantic and Northern Pacific Oceans. Their diet is fish, more specifically cod, flounder, herring, mackerel, rockfish, and salmon. These seals are able to dive down to depths of 500 feet. One of the longest dives was recorded at 1,460 feet. They breathe air, but they can stay under water for up to 30 minutes at a time. Male harbor seals weigh between 120 pounds to 300 pounds. Whereas the Females can weigh from 100 pounds to 190 pounds.

Grizzly Bear

  • About

    The Grizzly Bear is native to Western North America. Their diet includes berries, fish, fruit, leaves, nuts, rodents, and roots. Grizzly bears like to congregate together when the salmon swims upstream, which is called summer spawning. Surprisingly, grizzly bears can run up to 30 miles per hour even when an adult bear can weigh up to 800 pounds. These bears have lived for around 25 years, and the mother bears are very protective of their cubs.
