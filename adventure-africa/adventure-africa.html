Reticulated Giraffe

  • About

    Reticulated Giraffe’s are native to open grasslands of Sub-Saharan African. Their diet includes buds and leaves, and you can even feed them when visiting the zoo. Giraffes survive 5 to 30 minutes of sleep a day. They can rest while standing, but they do like to lie down. When they lay down, this puts them into a vulnerable position, so another giraffe will stay on guard for them. Reticulated Giraffes weigh from 1,750 to 2,800 pounds.

Hippopotamus

  • About

    Hippopotami are native to lakes, swamps, and slow-flowing rivers of Sub-Saharan Africa. The word “Hippopotamus” comes from a Greek word that means “river horse”, even though they are not related to horses. Their diet consists of grass. Male hippos can weigh from 3,500 pounds to 8,000 pounds. Whereas female hippos can weigh from 1,500 to 3,100 pounds. Hippos are able to hold their breath under water for up to 5 minutes. Their lifespan is 35 to 45 years.

African Savanna Elephant

  • About

    African Savanna Elephants are native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo, Kenya, Namibia, Sub-Saharan Africa, South Africa, Tanzania, Uganda, Zambia, and Zimbabwe. Their diet includes bark, fruit, grasses, herbs, leaves, shrubs, and vines. Elephants play a great role in the ecosystem of the African Savanna. When they move across the land, they clear the roots and trees as they feed them. This helps the savanna not be overgrown with grass and wood. Male elephants weigh around 5 to 6 tons and females weigh from 4 to 5 tons.
