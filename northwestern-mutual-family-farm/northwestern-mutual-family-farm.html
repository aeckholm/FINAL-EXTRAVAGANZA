Nigerian Dwarf Goat

  • About

    Nigerian Dwarf Goats are native to West Africa. Their diet consists of grass, grain, and hay. These goats used to be known as pygmy goats but are now know by the name of Nigerian Dwarf Goats. These goats have four different stomachs and are known as ruminants. This means they chew cud regurgitated from their first stomach. A doe Nigerian dwarf goat produces around a half gallon of milk each day. Adult dwarfs weigh from 25 to 75 pounds. Their lifespan is around 15 years.

North American Porcupine

  • About

    North American Porcupines are native to mixed-forest habitats of Canada, northern Mexico, and the northeastern and western regions of the United States. Their diet consists of berries, grasses, leaves, roots, seeds, and stems. Porcupines' quills are hollow; 2 to 3 inches in length, and they have about 30,000 of them. Adult porcupines weigh around 20 pounds and live around 18 years.

Scottish Highland

  • About

    The Scottish Highland is native to Scotland. Their diet consists of flowers, grass, and leaves. The highland breed is the oldest registered in the world. Their coat is long with hair that can reach 13 inches. They also have long horns that help them forage food in the winter. Adult Scottish Highlands weigh around 1,100 pounds and they live for about 20 years.
