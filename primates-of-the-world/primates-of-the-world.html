Red-Ruffed Lemur

  • About

    The red-ruffed lemur is native to the Masoala peninsula of Madagascar in the tropical rainforests. Their diet consits of fruit, leaves, nectar, and seeds. Red-ruffed lemurs are the world's largest lemurs, and they live in female-dominated family groups. These lemurs spend most of their time living in trees and rarely descend to the ground. They are mostly active during the day and spend these hours socializing and eating. They weigh from 7 to 10 pounds, and they live for about 15 to 20 years. Unfortunately, they are critically endangered.

Orangutan

  • About

    Orangutans are native to Indonesia and Malaysia on the islands of Borneo and Sumatra. Their diet consits of bark, flowers, fruits, leaves, and insects. Orangutans are the largest tree-dwelling animal. Their name means a person from the forest in Malay. They are very solitary animals and they rarley encounter other orangutans in the wild. The males weigh from around 200 to 250 pounds and the females weigh around 100 to 150 pounds. Their lifespan is 35 to 40 years. They are also unfortunately critically endangered.
